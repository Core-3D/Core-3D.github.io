Abstract

While Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have significantly advanced 3D spatial reasoning, extending them to dense 3D continuous sequences—such as multiparametric MRI or spatial video frames—triggers a catastrophic token explosion. This massive influx of redundant spatial tokens not only breaches computational and memory constraints but also degrades reasoning due to attention dilution.


We present Core3D, a plug-and-play, ultra-lightweight dynamic token pruning architecture driven by extreme computational compression. Core3D uses a dual-stage pipeline: Inter-Slice Feature Similarity Filtering and Dynamic Information Nucleus Selection. The framework prunes over 90% tokens, reduces VRAM usage by more than 80%, and accelerates inference—while improving spatial reasoning over baseline models.


Extensive experiments on medical and multi-view 3D benchmarks show that Core3D democratizes efficient 3D multimodal understanding for edge and clinical domains.
