Abstract

Despite the remarkable progress of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) in 3D spatial reasoning, extending them to dense 3D sequential data (e.g., multi-parametric MRI, spatial video frames) leads to a catastrophic token explosion problem. Massive redundant spatial tokens exceed the computational and memory limits of standard Transformers and degrade reasoning performance due to attention dilution.


To address these bottlenecks, we present Core3D, a plug-and-play, ultra-lightweight dynamic token pruning architecture designed with extreme computational compression as the core principle. Core3D integrates seamlessly into existing 3D-MLLMs via a two-stage routing layer: first, a cross-slice feature similarity filtering module eliminates redundant backgrounds between adjacent physical slices at a macro level; second, a dynamic information kernel selection mechanism adaptively preserves the most critical semantic kernels using self-attention matrices and performs hard pruning on non-informative features.


By optimizing a sparse regularization loss during parameter-efficient fine-tuning, the large-scale backbone remains fully frozen. Extensive evaluations on dense medical sequences (BraTS) and unconstrained multi-view environments (MV-ScanQA, SPATIALAB) show that Core3D achieves an extreme token compression rate of over 90%, reduces peak GPU memory usage by more than 80%, significantly accelerates inference, and outperforms unpruned baselines in 3D spatial localization and geometric reasoning. Core3D enables hardware-accelerated deployment of high-performance 3D-MLLMs for resource-constrained edge and clinical scenarios.
