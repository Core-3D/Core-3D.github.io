Abstract

While Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have significantly advanced 3D spatial reasoning, extending them to dense 3D continuous sequences, such as multiparametric MRI or spatial video frames, triggers a catastrophic token explosion. This massive influx of redundant spatial tokens not only breaches the computational and memory ceilings of standard Transformers but also paradoxically degrades reasoning performance due to attention dilution.


To overcome these bottlenecks, we introduce Core3D, a plug-and-play, ultra-lightweight dynamic token pruning architecture driven by the principle of extreme computational compression. Core3D seamlessly integrates into existing 3D-MLLMs via a dual-stage routing layer. First, an Inter-Slice Feature Similarity Filtering module aggressively truncates macro-level redundant backgrounds across adjacent physical slices. Second, a Dynamic Information Nucleus Selection mechanism leverages inherent self-attention matrices to adaptively preserve only the most critical semantic nuclei, effectively executing a hard prune on non-informative features.


By optimizing this framework with a sparsity regularization loss during parameter-efficient fine-tuning, the massive foundational backbones remain completely frozen. Extensive evaluations across dense medical sequences (BraTS) and unconstrained multi-view environments (MV-ScanQA, SPATIALAB) demonstrate that Core3D achieves an extreme token compression rate of over 90%. Consequently, it slashes peak VRAM consumption by over 80% and dramatically accelerates inference, while simultaneously surpassing unpruned baselines in 3D spatial localization and geometric reasoning. Core3D democratizes the hardware-accelerated deployment of powerful 3D-MLLMs for resource-constrained edge and clinical domains.
