Abstract

While Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have significantly advanced 3D spatial reasoning, extending them to dense 3D continuous sequences triggers a catastrophic token explosion. This massive influx of redundant spatial tokens breaches the computational and memory ceilings of standard Transformers and paradoxically degrades reasoning performance due to attention dilution[cite: 5, 6].


To overcome these bottlenecks, we introduce Core3D, a plug-and-play, ultra-lightweight dynamic token pruning architecture driven by the principle of extreme computational compression. Core3D integrates into existing 3D-MLLMs via a dual-stage routing layer: first, an Inter-Slice Feature Similarity Filtering module aggressively truncates macro-level redundant backgrounds; second, a Dynamic Information Nucleus Selection mechanism adaptively preserves only the most critical semantic nuclei via self-attention rollout[cite: 7, 10].


Extensive evaluations across dense medical sequences (BraTS) and multi-view environments demonstrate that Core3D achieves an extreme compression rate of over 90%, slashes peak VRAM by >80%, and surpasses unpruned baselines in spatial localization[cite: 12, 13]. Our framework democratizes the deployment of powerful 3D-MLLMs for resource-constrained edge and clinical domains[cite: 14].
